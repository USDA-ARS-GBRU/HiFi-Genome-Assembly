# v0.6 Telomere, Centromere, and T2T Kickoff

v0.6 focuses on chromosome-completeness evidence after chromosome-scale scaffolding. The goal is not to encourage overclaiming T2T assemblies. The goal is to help crop genome projects describe telomeres, centromeres, unresolved gaps, difficult repeats, and completeness claims with enough evidence for manuscript and database review.

v0.5 remains in content-review mode until citation/contributor wording and beginner usability review are complete. v0.6 can develop in parallel because it extends the existing telomere, gap, centromere, and T2T-readiness framework.

## Goals

- Expand telomere motif discovery and known-motif workflows.
- Add stronger `tidk` examples for known plant motifs and de novo motif discovery.
- Add `quarTeT` telomere and centromere examples.
- Define centromere evidence classes for crop assemblies.
- Create a completeness evidence package that connects FASTA, AGP, gap, telomere, centromere, contact-map, and dotplot evidence.
- Improve manuscript language for chromosome-scale, near-gapless, candidate T2T, and T2T-quality claims.
- Keep claims conservative when HiFi-only data cannot resolve long satellites or other complex repeats.

## Decision Vocabulary

Use specific terms:

| Term | Use when | Avoid when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| chromosome-scale | scaffolds represent expected chromosomes and are supported by Hi-C or comparable long-range evidence | chromosome ends, gaps, or centromeres are being claimed complete |
| near-gapless | most or all chromosome scaffolds have few or no unresolved Ns, with documented gap review | difficult repeats were not inspected |
| candidate T2T chromosome | one chromosome has no Ns, terminal telomere evidence on both ends, plausible centromere evidence, and no unresolved structural concerns | centromere or satellite repeats lack support |
| T2T-quality assembly | all expected chromosomes meet candidate T2T evidence and difficult repeats have spanning support | HiFi-only evidence leaves long repeats unresolved |

## Evidence Priorities

For crop genomes, prioritize independent evidence over a single tool call:

1. FASTA and AGP gap status.
2. Terminal and internal telomere motif evidence.
3. Centromere candidate intervals with repeat, Hi-C, or experimental support.
4. Hi-C contact-map continuity and orientation.
5. Dotplot review against related assemblies.
6. HiFi and, when available, ultra-long ONT or optical-map support through difficult repeats.
7. Contamination and organelle review.
